Rules of Mathematical Billiards

  1. Move in straight lines at constant speed inside the table.
  2. Reflect elastically at the boundary: “angle of incidence equals angle of reflection.”
  3. At a smooth boundary point, reflection flips the velocity component normal to the wall and keeps the tangential component unchanged.
  4. Corner hits are ambiguous (two walls at once). Most demos treat these as “stop / undefined.”

Two common viewpoints

  • Reflection viewpoint: stay in one table, flip direction at bounces.
  • Unfolding viewpoint: reflect the table instead, so the path becomes a straight line through tiled copies.
